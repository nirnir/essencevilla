§ 02Pool, Steam Room & Spa

Private · indoorAll-season

The pool

  1. A private, indoor pool — swimmable through every season.
  2. Arrange before you arrive and we'll heat or cool it to the temperature you'd like.
  3. The pool lights are left on, on purpose, 24/7.
Counter-currentSwim in place

The pool swimming jet

  1. The jet is set into the wall at the shallow end of the pool.
  2. Two buttons sit beside it: the right (black) button turns the jet on and off; the left button sets the strength.
  3. With the jet off, you can swivel the nozzle to send the water in either of two directions, whichever you prefer.
Please turn the jet off once you've finished swimming.
Wellness wing · Eucalyptus~45 °C

The steam room

  1. The on/off control is a red plug on the shower beside the steam room — switch it on there.
  2. From a cold start (unused for a while) it takes about 45 minutes to reach temperature; if it has been used recently, about 15.
  3. Please switch it off at the same red plug once you've finished.
Important — a spout inside the steam room releases scalding-hot steam. Keep your distance and never touch it with any part of your body.
Garden floorOpen all hours

The private gym

  1. A bicycle, a treadmill and a pulley (cable) machine, plus a bench and dumbbells.
  2. A portable Sonos speaker sits in the gym — switch it on for music.
  3. There's a TV to keep you company while you exercise.

§ 03The kitchen

Three machinesBeans · ground · capsule

The coffee machines

  1. Built-in, bean-to-cup. Grinds whole beans. The bean container is on top to the right; the water is a removable tank on the left. To slide the unit out for filling, pull the lever below the drip tray — gently, no force, and it comes forward.
  2. Smeg, for pre-ground. Tip already-ground coffee into the top and press the on button.
  3. Capsule espresso. Needs the matching capsules (buy the correct type). Drop one in and press your coffee on top of the machine.
Kitchen sinkWaste disposer

Sink garbage grinder

  1. Run cold water first, then flip the wall switch beside the sink to start the grinder.
  2. Feed soft food scraps in slowly; keep the water running a few seconds after.
  3. No bones, shells, fibrous peels, oil or anything non-food — those go in the caddy under the sink.
If it hums but won't turn, switch it off and tell Minho's Guest — please don't reach in.
Dishwasher · MieleTwo drawers

Dishwasher

  1. Pods are under the sink — one per drawer.
  2. It runs a fast eco mode or a longer mode — pick whichever suits the load.
  3. You can run just the bottom drawer, just the top drawer, or both together.

§ 04Televisions & sound

Samsung Smart TVs · Sonos

Every room has it. Every room hides it.

  1. One remote per room

    One Samsung remote runs everything in the room — power, volume, and the apps. That's the only one you need.

  2. Sign in, sign out

    Every TV is a Samsung smart TV — Netflix, HBO Max, Amazon Prime, YouTube, Apple TV and more are built in. Sign in with your own accounts, and sign out before you leave. We never see your credentials.

  3. Sonos — house-wide

    Twelve zones. Pull the Sonos app on any phone; the network appears as MatosGuest. The pool, terrace, and steam room have their own zones.

  4. Cinema sound

    The main living room has cinema-grade speakers, and the family room downstairs has a Sonos TV speaker. Both are already connected — when you watch TV or play games, the sound comes through them automatically. Nothing to set up.

§ 05Xbox & play

The playroom is for whoever lays claim to it first — by age or by guile.

  1. Powering on

    Press the Xbox button on the controller. The TV will switch to the right source automatically. A second controller is in the cabinet drawer.

  2. Your account

    Sign in to your own Xbox account to reach your games library, and please sign out before you leave.

§ 06The iRobots

Two iRobot vacuums keep the floors clean.

  1. Where they live

    One downstairs, next to the laundry room; one upstairs, next to the guest shower — each on its charging dock.

  2. To run one

    Just press the CLEAN button on top. It sets off on its own and returns to its dock when it's finished.

  3. If one gets stuck

    Lift it back onto its charging dock; it will recharge and reset itself, ready for the next run.

§ 07Climate & comfort

Radiant · hydronicPreset

Floor & ceiling heating

  1. Radiant heat runs through the floors and ceilings in the cooler months.
  2. The temperature is preset — it can't be changed from the room panels.
  3. Tell us the temperature you'd like before you arrive and we'll set it for your stay.
Radiant · hydronicPreset

Floor & ceiling cooling

  1. In summer the same radiant system cools through the floors and ceilings.
  2. It's preset too — not adjusted from the room panels.
  3. Let us know your preferred temperature before arrival; for a quicker chill, use the air conditioning.
Living room & main bedroomDaikin

Air conditioning

  1. Air conditioning is in the living room and the main bedroom — set the temperature on the room's panel.
  2. The other bedrooms have fans for now; air conditioning is being added throughout.
  3. Please close the windows while the air conditioning is running.

§ 08Water & washrooms

Its own machineRoom temp & boiling

Filtered water

  1. Filtered water comes from its own machine at the sink — not the ordinary tap.
  2. It pours room-temperature or boiling water on demand — no kettle needed.
The boiling setting is genuinely hot — take care, and keep little ones clear.
Master · Guest · Quiet3-mode

The showers

  1. The master suite, the guest shower and the Quiet Suite each have a three-mode shower.
  2. The dial switches between the head from above, the rain shower, and the three body jets.
  3. The lever sets the temperature — run it a moment to settle and it holds steady.
